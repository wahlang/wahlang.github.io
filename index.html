About my reasearch :

As theorists, we know a lot about the behaviour of atoms and elementary particles such as electrons, protons, and neutrons. Also, how they respond to perturbations and external forces such as electric and magnetic fields. The problem arises and things become more complicated when we have many of them together (such as electrons in solids). These systems, when we cool down the temperature close to absolute zero, they behave differently from what we usually see at room temperature. By cooling to such temperature, the system's interactions will dominate over the kinetic fluctuations; hence, we say they are strongly coupled systems. One such system is the High-temperature superconductors (HTSC).

Depending on the doping on these materials, we have different phases, e.g., pseudogap phase, antiferromagnetic phase, Mott phase, non-Fermi liquid etc,. Over the last two,- three decades, high temperature superconductors and other new materials were discovered. Many of the transport properties in these materials are determined by the structure of the energy band and a closed Fermi surface. However, experiments such as Angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES) perform on HTSC, Topological insulators , Weyl and Dirac semi-metals revealed many interesting results and strange phenomena. For example, the Fermi surface that partially closed, which we call Fermi arcs, the presence of the complicated surface states connecting the valence and conduction energy bands to name a few. These Fermi arcs seen in the pseudogap phase of HTSC and semimetals are exactly our interest. We mainly construct a theoretical model to come up with an explanation in understanding the underlying mechanism. We do this not by using the conventional method that condensed matter physicists would use but by using one of the remarkable duality, formulated in 1997 known as AdS/CFT Correspondence.
